Blue Ticket System for Bicycles in Japan

Even without a driver’s license, you are a road user.
Please follow traffic rules and ride your bicycle responsibly.

Who does this system apply to?

This system applies to bicycle riders aged 16 and over. From April 1, 2026, certain bicycle traffic violations will be subject to the Blue Ticket (Traffic Infraction Notification Procedure).

Basic policy of guidance and enforcement

When a bicycle traffic violation is detected, the police will, in principle, provide guidance and warnings on the spot.

However, dangerous or malicious violations that may cause traffic accidents or pose a danger or nuisance to pedestrians or other vehicles may be subject to enforcement measures.

This basic policy does not change with the introduction of the Blue Ticket system.

What is the Traffic Infraction Notification Procedure (Blue Ticket)?

When a bicycle rider aged 16 or over commits a violation covered by this procedure, a Blue Ticket will be issued.

The Blue Ticket describes the violation and includes a notice to pay a fixed fine.

  • If the fine is paid:
    the case is closed without criminal proceedings and no criminal record.
  • If the fine is not paid:
    the case may proceed to criminal procedures.

This procedure applies only to violations that police officers directly witness and can clearly confirm, such as running a red light or failing to stop where required.

Serious violations (Red Ticket)

Serious violations are not handled by the Blue Ticket system and are subject to criminal prosecution.

Examples of serious violations include:

  • Riding under the influence of alcohol
  • Dangerous or obstructive riding
  • Using a mobile phone in a way that causes traffic danger
  • Hit-and-run incidents

Examples of violations and fines

12,000 yen

  • Using a mobile phone while holding it in your hand

7,000 yen

  • Entering a railroad crossing while the gate is closing or closed

6,000 yen

  • Running a red light
  • Failing to follow safe riding obligations
  • Riding against traffic or riding improperly on sidewalks
  • Obstructing pedestrians or other cyclists

5,000 yen

  • Failing to stop at designated stopping points
  • Riding without a light
  • Riding a bicycle with defective brakes

3,000 yen

  • Riding side by side
  • Violating passenger or loading restrictions (for example, riding with two people)

Mandatory bicycle rider training

If a person repeatedly commits certain dangerous violations, the Public Safety Commission may order the person to attend bicycle rider training.

Failure to attend the training may result in a fine of up to 50,000 yen.

Examples of dangerous behavior:

  • Running a red light
  • Failing to stop where required
  • Using a mobile phone while riding
  • Violating traffic rules
